Working Student: Mechanical Engineering for Experimental Testing

At Muniqo Performante, we are developing an innovative anti-vibration system for the transport of premature and critically ill patients. We combine lightweight design methods with classical control engineering and machine learning.

Our goal: improve medical care, create real societal impact, and develop a universally applicable technology.

For our government-funded validation project, we are looking for a Working Student in Mechanical Engineering (m/f/d) for Experimental Testing.

Your Tasks

  • Planning and execution of exploratory validation tests
  • Construction, development, and optimization of hardware prototypes
  • Mechanical work on magnetic assemblies
  • Design & rapid prototyping (3D printing, CNC)
  • Thermal analysis & FEM simulations (SolidWorks, COMSOL, Simulia)
  • Collaboration with embedded and control engineers

What You Bring

Technical Skills

  • Experience in hardware design and experimental testing
  • Good CAD skills (SolidWorks)
  • Basic understanding of control engineering & microcontrollers (STM32, Arduino)
  • Laboratory experience and high precision
  • Fluent in German (C1)

Personal Skills

  • Hands-on mentality & pragmatism
  • Structured way of working
  • Teamwork & strong communication skills
  • Interest in MedTech & societal impact
  • Willingness to commit 15 hours per week

What We Offer

We offer an agile startup environment with flat hierarchies, flexible working hours, hybrid work options, and the opportunity to take early responsibility.

You will work on a system that uniquely combines hardware and software and creates real impact — with a genuine perspective for a future leadership role.
