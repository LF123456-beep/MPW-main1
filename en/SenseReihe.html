VibraSense · Workflow

Precise vibration measurement in four steps.

01

Place & Start Recording

Switch VibraSense on wirelessly, fix it to the measurement object — recording starts automatically.

02

Remove SD Card

After the measurement is complete, switch off the device and remove the SD card with all logged data.

03

Upload File to Computer

Connect the SD card to your laptop and transfer the measurement file to the software.

04

Start Analysis

Open the software, upload the file and launch the automated analysis of all recorded data with a single click.

Maximum Acceleration

The measuring range defines the maximum detectable acceleration. Larger ranges reduce sensitivity — choose the smallest range that covers your expected peak values.

Measurement Duration

How long should the box operate autonomously? Battery capacity and storage determine the maximum runtime.

Live Readout of Multiple Boxes

How many boxes should be read simultaneously in real time? More parallel streams require higher network bandwidth.

What is the approximate frequency of your application?

Lower frequencies provide greater sensitivity for fine vibration. Higher frequencies capture faster events but may be slightly less sensitive. This selection does not affect the price.

Required Precision

Defines the resolution and noise floor of the measurements. Higher precision requires better components and calibration.

Precision and Ruggedness

Choose between maximum measurement quality and mechanical resilience for harsh environments.

Storage and Software Modules

32 GB is the standard. 64 GB is available for longer recordings. Live mode, spectrogram, GPS, and temperature can be added as modules.
